Chitranna

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal, chana dal
  • Handful of peanuts
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, dals, and peanuts. Sauté until golden.
  2. Add green chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida.
  3. Add turmeric powder and turn off the heat.
  4. Pour this tempering over the cooked rice. Add lemon juice and salt.
  5. Mix gently until well combined. Garnish with coriander.

Hayagreeva Maddi

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup chana dal (Bengal gram)
  • 1/2 cup jaggery, grated
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp cashews & raisins
  • 1/4 cup grated dry coconut (kopra)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1.5 cups water

Instructions

  1. Pressure cook chana dal with 1.5 cups of water until soft but not mushy.
  2. In a pan, melt jaggery with a little water to form a syrup.
  3. Add the cooked dal to the jaggery syrup and cook until it thickens.
  4. In another small pan, heat ghee and fry cashews and raisins until golden.
  5. Add the fried nuts, coconut, and cardamom powder to the dal mixture. Mix well and serve.

Kosambari

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup moong dal, soaked for 2 hours
  • 1 cucumber, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 2 tbsp fresh coconut, grated
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt to taste
  • For tempering: 1 tsp oil, 1/2 tsp mustard seeds, pinch of asafoetida

Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked moong dal completely.
  2. In a bowl, combine dal, cucumber, carrot, coconut, and green chili.
  3. Prepare tempering by heating oil and popping mustard seeds. Add asafoetida.
  4. Pour the tempering over the dal mixture. Add salt and lemon juice.
  5. Mix everything well and serve immediately.

Shavige Payasa

Ingredients

  • 1 cup roasted vermicelli (shavige)
  • 1 litre full-fat milk
  • 3/4 cup sugar (or to taste)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 10-12 cashews
  • 1 tbsp raisins
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder

Instructions

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee and fry cashews and raisins until golden. Set aside.
  2. If using un-roasted vermicelli, fry it in the same ghee until golden.
  3. Boil the milk in a separate pot. Once it boils, add the roasted vermicelli.
  4. Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the vermicelli is soft.
  5. Add sugar and stir until it dissolves completely.
  6. Finally, add cardamom powder and the fried nuts. Serve warm or chilled.

Mysore Pak

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Sieve the besan to ensure no lumps.
  2. In a pan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil to make a one-string consistency syrup.
  3. Slowly add the besan while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Once mixed, start adding melted ghee little by little, while constantly stirring.
  5. Continue cooking until the mixture becomes frothy and leaves the sides of the pan.
  6. Pour immediately onto a greased tray. Let it cool slightly before cutting into squares.

Obbattu / Holige

Ingredients

  • For filling: 1 cup toor dal, 1 cup jaggery, 1/4 tsp cardamom powder.
  • For dough: 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida), pinch of turmeric, 3 tbsp oil, water.

Instructions

  1. Cook dal until soft. Drain and mash with jaggery. Cook this mixture until it thickens, then add cardamom. Let it cool.
  2. Make a soft, pliable dough with maida, turmeric, oil, and water. Let it rest for 2 hours.
  3. Make small balls of the filling and dough.
  4. Stuff a filling ball inside a dough ball, seal, and roll it out thinly like a chapati.
  5. Cook on a hot tawa with ghee until golden brown on both sides.

Puliyogare

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • Small lime-sized tamarind ball
  • 2 tbsp Puliyogare powder/paste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • Handful of peanuts
  • Few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak tamarind in hot water and extract thick juice.
  2. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, peanuts, and curry leaves. Sauté until peanuts are crisp.
  3. Add tamarind extract and boil for a few minutes.
  4. Add the Puliyogare powder/paste and salt. Cook until the mixture thickens into a paste.
  5. Let the paste cool, then mix it thoroughly with the cooked rice.

Masala Vada

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chana dal, soaked for 2 hours
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • Few curry leaves & coriander
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Drain the dal and grind it coarsely without water.
  2. Mix the ground dal with onion, chilies, ginger, herbs, fennel seeds, and salt.
  3. Shape the mixture into small, flat patties.
  4. Deep fry in hot oil on medium heat until golden and crispy on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney.

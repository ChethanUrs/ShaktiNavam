A Journey Through Time

1610 CE

The first Dasara festivities were inaugurated by Raja Wodeyar I at Srirangapatna, marking the victory of good over evil.

1799 CE

After the death of Tipu Sultan, Krishnaraja Wodeyar III moved the capital back to Mysuru, reviving the festival's grandeur and traditions.

Early 20th Century

Under HH Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV, the festival gained international fame. The iconic Jumboo Savari (elephant procession) became its magnificent centerpiece.

Present Day

Mysuru Dasara is celebrated as 'Nada Habba' (state-festival), organized by the Government of Karnataka, preserving its rich cultural heritage for the world.
